Autonomous Path-Finding Rover

Exploring unknown terrain, mapping obstacles, and finding optimal routes using the A* algorithm.

Short Project Description

This project turns the TI-RSLK kit into a fully autonomous path-finding robot. Using an HC-SR04 ultrasonic sensor mounted on a servo, the bot first explores an unknown grid, labelling every cell as walkable, blocked, or unknown while updating a live grid on the SSD1306 OLED display. In Route mode, the robot runs an A* search in real-time, renders the optimal path on the OLED, then drives the course until it reaches its target and halts.

Original Conceptualization

The idea grew out of a series of labs on robotic motion and a desire to merge those experiences with the embedded-systems skills developed throughout the semester. Early sketches called for a basic wall-following routine, but a design review pivoted the plan toward dynamic mapping plus classic heuristic search so the robot could adapt to changing environments instead of memorizing a fixed maze.

Obstacles Faced

  • A* implementation: Porting the algorithm from Python to C and integrating it into a limited-memory microcontroller.
  • Explore & Route coordination: Ensuring the routing phase never started until the exploration maps were both complete and reliable.
  • Servo calibration: A burned-out MSP432 forced a board swap, requiring fresh PWM calibration for precise left, right, and center angles.
  • Dead-ends & backtracking: Robust logic was needed for dead-end detection, backtracking, and alternate-path discovery.

Testing & Verification

  • Sensor range tests: Cardboard walls at varying distances validated ultrasonic-sensor thresholds.
  • Motion calibration: A mock grid confirmed forward-move distances and turning PWMs.
  • Edge-case mazes: Purpose-built dead-end and blocked-path layouts stress-tested the explore logic.
  • Route trials: Simple and complex mazes demonstrated that A* always delivered the optimal path.

Possible Improvements

  • User-defined target coordinates for truly flexible missions.
  • Merge explore and route into one loop to enable dynamic re-planning when new obstacles appear.
  • Enable diagonal movement for more efficient A* paths.
  • Support dynamic grid sizing so the rover can tackle arenas larger than the current 5 × 5 layout.
